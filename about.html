About GTTI Gulberg Lahore

Building skilled professionals for Pakistan's technological future since 1934

Our Story

The current Technical Training Institute was initially established as the British Technical Training School in Lahore in 1934. It was renamed as the Technical Training Institute in 1962 and has since operated under the Ministry of Defence, providing skilled training in various trades.

The institute offers training in 24 trades, with a capacity of 1043 trainees. Currently, 762 male and 281 female students are enrolled. In 1972, the Girls Wing was added to provide technical training to women as well.

This institution has consistently produced skilled professionals who are serving in Pakistan and abroad. With modern workshops, experienced instructors, and updated courses, the institute plays a key role in national development.
